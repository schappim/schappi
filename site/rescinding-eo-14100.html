The Impact of Rescinding Executive Order 14110: What It Means for AI Governance

On January 20, 2025, within hours of assuming office, President Donald Trump rescinded Executive Order 14110, a comprehensive directive issued by President Joe Biden in October 2023 to establish a national framework for the governance of artificial intelligence (AI). The move raises critical questions about the future of AI policy in the United States. Here's a look at what this decision means in practical terms.

What Was Executive Order 14110?

Executive Order 14110, titled Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy Development and Use of Artificial Intelligence, was designed to guide the development, deployment, and governance of AI in the United States. It outlined a series of ambitious goals, including:

  • Promoting competition and innovation in the AI industry.
  • Protecting civil liberties and consumer rights from AI-enabled threats.
  • Strengthening national security through AI safeguards.
  • Developing tools like watermarking systems to identify AI-generated content.

The order also required federal agencies to appoint chief AI officers and tasked them with creating AI-specific guidelines and policies. Agencies such as the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) were given specific responsibilities, ranging from improving cybersecurity to managing AI risks.

The Practical Implications of Rescinding the Order

Revoking this order has significant ramifications for AI governance and the broader AI landscape. Here’s what the change means:

  1. Halted Agency Obligations Agencies are no longer mandated to comply with the order’s requirements, including appointing chief AI officers or developing AI-specific guidelines. Initiatives such as AI competitions for reducing healthcare worker burnout or AI-related risk management frameworks may come to a standstill.
  2. Reduced Federal Coordination on AI The order provided a unified framework for addressing AI challenges, such as bias, misinformation, and national security risks. Without it, federal agencies may develop inconsistent approaches to AI governance, leaving critical gaps in oversight and regulation.
  3. Uncertainty in AI Regulation The removal of a central directive creates ambiguity in how AI technologies should be regulated. Key issues such as privacy protections, intellectual property rights for AI-generated content, and the ethical use of AI may remain unaddressed, leaving room for industry confusion and public concern.
  4. Potential Shifts in AI Funding Priorities Executive Order 14110 prioritised investment in federal AI research and development. With the order rescinded, funding and focus may shift elsewhere, potentially slowing innovation and weakening public-sector contributions to the AI ecosystem.
  5. A Return to Earlier Policies or a New Approach Rescinding the order may signal a return to Trump-era AI policies or the introduction of a new framework. The direction the administration takes will determine whether AI development aligns with global standards or diverges toward deregulation.

Why Does This Matter?

The rapid growth of AI technologies requires robust, adaptable governance to ensure safety, fairness, and innovation. Executive Order 14110 aimed to strike this balance by addressing immediate risks while fostering long-term opportunities. Its repeal leaves a void in federal AI policy, potentially affecting global competitiveness and public trust in AI systems.

Looking Ahead

The decision to rescind Executive Order 14110 is a pivotal moment for U.S. AI policy. While the Trump administration may introduce new AI-related measures, the absence of a comprehensive framework creates uncertainty for federal agencies, private industry, and civil society.

As the AI landscape evolves, the need for clear, actionable governance remains more pressing than ever. Whether through legislation, new executive action, or international cooperation, the future of AI policy in the United States hinges on balancing innovation with responsible oversight.

This development underscores the importance of keeping an eye on federal policies and their broader implications. As the story unfolds, it will shape not only the AI industry but also the lives of citizens who interact with these transformative technologies.
